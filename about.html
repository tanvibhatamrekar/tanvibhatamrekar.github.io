About Guru Padmaja Kelam

Guru Padmaja takes each and every student through an individualized “Margam” of sorts as she instills in them, through a blend of love and nurturance, an understanding of the exalted art form in its breathtaking glory, while imbuing a lifelong commitment to a spiritual journey that cannot be otherwise replicated.

Her brilliant ability to juxtapose tradition and innovation is seen in every riveting masterpiece that she creates with loving and effortless detail with delicate nuances, bringing a whole new meaning to the phrase “Poetry in motion”.

Padmaja’s belief in every child being able to reach individualized pinnacles of perfection is embodied in her demand for perfection in the execution of mudras, adavus, bhedas, and karanas while striving to build and hone the endurance to perform flawlessly with utmost dedication and devotion.

Under her tutelage, several students have experienced the beauty and divinity of their “Arangetrams”. Her 50th Arangetram in 2017 only served to project the many more that are yet to come.

Guru Padmaja is indeed one of Atlanta’s artistic gems, bestowing sheer joy and sublime delight in her students as they walk the paths of their dance “margams” with seamless eloquence and precision.

About Tanvi Bhatamrekar

Tanvi Bhatamrekar, a disciple of the esteemed Guru Padmaja Kelam, carries forward the legacy of classical dance with grace and dynamism. Her performances are marked by technical precision and soulful expression, reflecting years of rigorous training and a deep commitment to the art of Bharatanatyam.

Vocal

K. Hari Prasad

Kaniyal Hari Prasad was drawn to classical music at the age of six under the training of Sri Govindabhagavathar. Along with his sister, he gave concerts since 1980 at local temples which were highly appreciated. Later he pursued a prestigious diploma course in Carnatic Vocal at Kalakshetra College of Fine Arts under Vidvan Sri D. Chellamayyanagar, and also learned under Vidvan Sri Vairamangalam Lakshmi Narayanan and Sri S. Rajaram (grandson of Sri Mysore Vasudevachar). Gifted to learn compositions of Dr. Sri M. Balamuralikrishna, he toured the US with him and has also trained under Sri V. Subrahmanyam. For the past 37 years, he has accompanied dance as a vocalist.

Flute

B. Muthukumar

A third-generation musician whose father, Adyar Balu, was a renowned mridangist. Muthukumar began his training under Mayavaram Saraswathi Ammal and then continued under internationally famous flute maestro Dr. N. Ramani for over two decades. In addition to performing with his five-flute ensemble, he is a frequent solo performer on the Indian stage and an A Grade artist of All India Radio, Chennai.

Mridangam

N.K. Kesavan

One of the most respected and popular percussionists for dance, Kesavan’s brilliant innovations make him a creative artist par excellence. Proficient in instruments like the Mridangam, Kanjira, Chenda, and Madhalam, he has collaborated with renowned film music directors including Ilayaraja and A.R. Rahman.
