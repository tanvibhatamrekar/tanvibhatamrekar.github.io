Program

Mallari

Raaga: Ghambiranattai

Mallari is an invocatory dance traditionally performed at the beginning of a margam. It sets a ceremonial tone, as the dancer symbolically leads a deity in procession, evoking a sense of reverence and celebration. The piece then transitions into a sequence of adavus—basic steps—performed in three increasing speeds, showcasing the dancer’s control, rhythm, and grace as they prepare the stage for the rest of the performance.

Sankeerna Allaripu

Allaripu is a traditional opening dance that symbolizes the dancer’s physical and spiritual awakening. With complex sankeerna jaati and rhythmic head, neck, and shoulder movements that gradually expand into full-body expressions, this piece allows the dancer to tune into the rhythm and build focus. Though abstract, it is full of grace, control, and elegance.

Jathiswaram

Raaga: Nasika Bhuhanam (Aditalam)

This vibrant piece is all about movement and rhythm. With no narrative or expression, Jathiswaram highlights the dancer’s technique, precision, and ability to keep perfect timing. Complex footwork and crisp poses create beautiful visual patterns, showcasing the dancer’s strength and training.

Simhavahini Varnam

Raaga: Rangini (Aditalam)

The Varnam is the centerpiece of the arangetram, combining both expressive storytelling and intricate rhythm. This piece is a heartfelt offering to the divine feminine, praising the three goddesses—Saraswati, Lakshmi, and Parvati—who represent wisdom, wealth, and strength. Through graceful abhinaya and dynamic footwork, the dancer brings out the unique qualities of each Devi while expressing deep devotion and technical precision.

Padam - Chaliye Kunjanu

Raaga: Vrindavanasaranga (Aditalam)

This expressive piece paints a beautiful scene of love and yearning, as a devotee eagerly waits for Lord Krishna to meet her in the sacred groves, only for Krishna to tease her that he won’t come. Through subtle expressions and graceful gestures, the dancer brings this lighthearted exchange to life, revealing mischief, longing, and affection in a quiet, intimate moment.

Nadarmudi

Raaga: Punnagavaialu (Aditalam)

A dramatic and captivating piece, Nadarmudi is a Naga dance that honors the mystical serpent beings often associated with divine power and protection. The choreography reflects the fluid, coiled movements of a serpent, blending grace with intensity to create a striking visual and emotional impact.

Tillana

Raaga: Nasika Bhushaban (Aditalam)

The Saraswati Tillana is a joyful and energetic conclusion to the recital, dedicated to the goddess Saraswati—the embodiment of knowledge, music, and the arts. Filled with brisk footwork, vibrant poses, and graceful movements, this piece celebrates the divine inspiration behind dance and leaves the audience uplifted and joyful.

Mangalam

The Abhang brings the performance to a devotional close, offering gratitude and spiritual reflection. Centered around Sant Eknath, this piece of bhakti invites the audience to end the evening in a spirit of peace, joy, and reverence.
